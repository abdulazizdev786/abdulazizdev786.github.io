Executive
Summary

The LBS Impact Study is an outcome assessment research conducted annually on LBS graduates to ascertain the effectiveness of its programmes on various measures of career success.

Consequently, this research aims to evaluate the resultant effect of the programme on career growth of the alumni measured through certain job performance indicators such as change in income, job performance, organisation/ business profitability, promotion, job satisfaction and the social impact of the institution and its graduates. There are various ways through which the graduates can make an impact in the society; PSR/ CSR (personal/ corporate social responsibility) activities, their roles as individuals, managers and essential members of their community.

These evaluations and research will help generate recommendations on how LBS can increase its impact in the future.

Previous impact studies measured the effectiveness of just the LBS MBA programme on career growth and advancement of the graduates. This year, the research was expanded to include measuring the effectiveness of the LBS Executive Education programmes and also to get in depth information about the social impact activities the graduates are currently involved in. This was deemed necessary to ascertain the impact of the LBS programmes on the lives and careers of the participants from a holistic view.

Designing
the Research

Methodology: The questionnaires were developed, reviewed and approved by the Directors of MBA and Executive Education programmes, and subsequently administered online via google form to the alumni in April, 2020. The survey was open for 8 weeks.

Sample: The survey was sent out to all 549 participants of the classes under review in the following order;

151 graduates of the FTMBA 8, 9 and 10 classes

120 graduates of the EMBA 14, 15 and 16 classes

45 graduates of the MEMBA 1 and 2 classes

48 graduates of the OMP 21 and 22 classes

111 graduates of the SMP 62, 63 and 64 classes

74 graduates of the AMP 26 and 27 classes

At least, 70% response was attained for each of the cohorts.

Data: The class profiles of the classes reviewed were obtained from the programme managers. The survey responses were collated, evaluated, analysed, tabulated and the results summarised below.

Implications

Following the results of the analyses gathered, we arrived at the following:

1

The LBS programme structure is robust and designed to meet the various needs of the participants who enroll on any of the programmes.

2.

Evidence shows that enrolling on any of the LBS programmes will lead to a significant rise in income/ annual revenue level.

3.

Participants are guaranteed to acquire the relevant knowledge and skills to help them improve, manage and progress in their career/ business.

4.

The programmes offered in the LBS create high self-awareness and promote greater confidence in the participants.

5.

Participants during the programme at LBS and upon completion are involved in CSR/ PSR activities, thereby play a huge role of social impact in the society.

6.

There is evidently a positive correlation with the LBS programmes and productivity, business profitability, sales and return on investment.

7.

Participants get to be trained by very professional and highly knowledgeable faculty members.

8.

The participants on all LBS programmes get international experience and exposure to high quality networking experience.

9.

Participants become more ethical in doing business and at work, and also create positive social impact in their community and the society at large.
